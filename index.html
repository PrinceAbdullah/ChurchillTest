Winston Churchill

The man who changed World War II

Churchill has served in almost every office of state in the UK. He is most known for his time as Prime Minister during the 2nd World War, but he was of significant value during the 1st World War where he served as Minister of Munitions. He was the one who started the development of the what would become the first armored vehicle, known today as tanks.

Political Carreer:

  • President of the board of Trade (1908-1910)
  • Home Secretary (1910-1911)
  • First Lord of the Admiralty (1911-1915)
  • Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster (1915-1915)
  • Minister of Munitions (1917-1919)
  • Secretary of State for War (1919-1921)
  • Secretary of State for Air (1919-1921)
  • Secretary of State for the Colonies (1921-1922)
  • Chancellor of the Exchequer (1924-1929)
  • First Lord of the Admiralty (1939-1940)
  • Prime Minister of the United Kingdom (1940-1945)
  • Minister of Defence (1951-1952)
  • Prime Minister of the United Kingdom (1951-1955

He has also won a Nobel Prize in Literature and has sold one of his paintings for 1 million pounds. He is an extraordinary man who achieved a lot in his lifetime. His political carreer was incredible, but his personal life was nothing too be proud of.
